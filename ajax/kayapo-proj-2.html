Territorial Monitoring & Control

Government authorities are largely absent as far as enforcing protected areas including indigenous territories in the Amazon. The NGO Alliance with the Kayapo provides the Kayapo organizations with transportation (boats, motors, trucks, fuel, equipment maintenance, overflight surveillance) and communications (short wave radios) they need to patrol their borders. The alliance is also trying to provide GIS satellite imagery and technology for remote monitoring.

Satellite and over-flight monitoring that allows the Kayapo to see borders and pin point invasions.

Capacity-building training courses in territorial patrol techniques

Transportation and communications infrastructure, fuel and other supplies for border surveillance expeditions

Sustainable Small Enterprise Development

after 50 years of increasing contact, the Kayapo have come to depend on certain manufactured goods from outside society such as metal tools, guns, motors, fuel, glass beads and clothes. Loggers and goldminers often try to take advantage of this need by pressuring Kayapo individuals to allow illegal access to timber and gold on their lands in exchange for cash. The Kayapo jointly decided they wanted no involvement with illegal predatory activities but instead they wanted to learn how to make their own money to buy the things they need themselves. Therefore, the NGO alliance helps the Kayapo to establish their own small enterprises based on non-timber forest products that can be harvested sustainably and sold by all members of a community who choose to (eg, brazil nut, cumaru seed, jamborandi leaves, field courses for international students, handicrafts). Developing their own small businesses that fit with their culture and natural environment, the Kayapo maintain their independence.

Economic autonomy based on sustainable management of nontimber forest resources such as brazil nut

Building capacity of local Kayapo institutions that are necessary for accessing and managing outside support for conservation and development.
